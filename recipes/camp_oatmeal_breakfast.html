Camping Oatmeal Breakfast

For an easy, no mess camping breakfast!

Taken from my motorcycle travels, this recipe for oatmeal has been my go-to for an easily constructed meal that is ready to eat in minutes, provides the right amount of calories and nutrition, and cleans up easily.

You could easily modify the ingredients to suit your tastes, as this combination of ingredients may not be for everyone. When traveling, I'm not overly concerned with taste - "food is fuel" is the main objective, along with avoiding processed foods and needing to stop along the way to purchase food.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup oatmeal, old fashioned cut
  • 1/8 tsp nutmeg, ground
  • 1/8 tsp ginger, ground
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon, ground
  • 1 tsp cocoa powder
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1 banana
  • 1 lemon
  • 1x mylar bag, 0.35 gal size
  • 0.25 L boiling water

Equipment needed

  • Food dehydrator
  • Vacuum sealer
  • 1 bowl

Steps

  1. Prepping the bananas
    1. NOTE: You certainly could avoid this step by buying dehydrated banana slices, if you don't have a dehydrator; also, I suggest buying a bundle of bananas and doing all at once, freezing the remainder of what you don't use.
    2. Juice the lemon into a bowl.
    3. Slice the bananas into small slices, around 1/8 of an inch.
    4. Briefly mix the slided bananas in with the lemon juice to coat them - this prevents browning of the banana.
    5. Load banana slices into the dehydrator.
    6. Set dehydrator temperature for 135F, and dehydrate for 10-12 hours.
    7. Before storing or using right away, measure the weight of the bananas and divide by the number of bananas used to obtain your serving weight for one banana.
    8. Once done, I store in mason jars in the freezer when not using.
  2. Constructing the meal
    1. Open up the mylar bag.
    2. Load the bag with the ingredients in this order: oatmeal, spices, cocoa, and chia seeds. Try to cover the cocoa powder in the chia seeds to prevent the cocoa dust from flying out.
    3. Load the bag with 1 banana's worth of dehydrated banana slices.
    4. Load the bag with the raisins.
    5. Seal the bag with the vacuum sealer. Oxygen absorbers may be more necessary for longer term storage or trips, but I haven't had a need for them.
  3. Serving the meal
    1. Open the mylar bag.
    2. Boil the water (I use a Jetboil exclusively for boiling water).
    3. Pour the water into the bag, stir, and close to let the water be absorbed for 5-10 minutes.
    4. Once the consistency is to your liking, enjoy!
    5. TIP: use the empty bag for small trash around the campsite and discard when you leave camp.
