Travel Lunch

Boring but good!

This is my go-to for lunch while traveling via motorcycle. It's difficult to find healthy food out on the roads, especially as you travel to more rural places.

The general struture is to obtain a reasonable ratio of protein, fat, and carbs to sustain yourself while travel, while cutting down on prep time and waste, and not needing to worry about spoilage. If I have time I'll make into a wrap, but lately I've lost all modesty and won't even bother with that... read on for more...

Ingredients

  • Meat - looking for 4-6 oz of beef jerky or hard salami - something that won't spoil easily.
  • Carb - whole wheat tortilla, flour tortilla - these are energy dense and pack well. Smaller the better I've found.
  • Cheese - usually 4oz of an aged white cheddar - kind of an indulgence, try to eat it within a day of opening.
  • Tomato - helps add some moisture to an otherwise dry meal

Steps

Two ways to eat this - sandwich wrap style, or barbarian style.

  1. Sandwich wrap
    1. Cut up ingredients to fit into tortilla.
    2. Wrap up and eat!
    3. 3-4 sandwiches is plenty filling for a lunchtime meal.
  2. Barbarian style - who has time to be cute with making a sandwich when there's adventure to be had?
    1. Unwrap ingredients.
    2. Take individual bites of one ingredient until satisfied.
    3. Move to the next ingredient and repeat.
